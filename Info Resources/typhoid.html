Overview

Typhoid fever is a life-threatening illness caused by the bacterium Salmonella Typhi. It is usually spread through contaminated food or water.

Key Facts

  • Caused by Salmonella Typhi
  • 11-21 million cases occur worldwide each year
  • Causes an estimated 128,000-161,000 deaths each year
  • Most common in areas with poor sanitation
  • Can be prevented with vaccination

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Typhoid Fever include:

  • High fever
  • Headache
  • Stomach pain
  • Constipation or diarrhea
  • Loss of appetite
  • Rash
  • Weakness and fatigue

Causes & Risk Factors

Typhoid fever is caused by Salmonella Typhi bacteria. The bacteria are passed on by eating food or drinking water that has been contaminated by someone who is infected or who is a carrier of the disease.

Treatment

Typhoid fever is treated with antibiotics. Drug resistance is increasingly common, so your doctor may need to order special tests to determine which strain of bacteria is causing your infection.
