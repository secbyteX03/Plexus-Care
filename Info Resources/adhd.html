Understanding ADHD

What is ADHD?

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder that affects both children and adults. It's characterized by patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with functioning and development.

Types of ADHD

Inattentive Type: Difficulty focusing and organizing
Hyperactive-Impulsive Type: Restlessness and impulsive behaviors
Combined Type: Features of both types

Who Does It Affect?

ADHD affects approximately 5-10% of children and 2.5-4% of adults worldwide. It's more commonly diagnosed in boys during childhood, but girls may be underdiagnosed.

Getting Diagnosed

Step 1: Recognize the Signs

Symptoms must be present for at least 6 months and interfere with daily functioning in multiple settings (home, work, school).

Step 2: Consult a Healthcare Professional

See a psychiatrist, psychologist, or specialized healthcare provider who can conduct a comprehensive evaluation.

Step 3: Comprehensive Assessment

Includes medical history, symptom questionnaires, psychological tests, and sometimes computer-based assessments.

Step 4: Rule Out Other Conditions

Healthcare providers will check for other conditions that might mimic ADHD symptoms, such as anxiety, depression, or learning disabilities.

Step 5: Develop Treatment Plan

Work with your healthcare team to create a personalized treatment approach that may include medication, therapy, and lifestyle changes.

💊

Treatment Options

Medications

Stimulants: Methylphenidate, Amphetamines
Non-Stimulants: Atomoxetine, Bupropion
Work with your doctor to find the right medication and dosage for you.

Behavioral Therapy

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) helps develop coping strategies, improve organization skills, and manage time more effectively.

Lifestyle Modifications

Regular exercise, adequate sleep, healthy diet, stress management, and structured routines can significantly improve symptoms.

ADHD Coaching

Professional coaches help develop practical skills for managing daily challenges, improving productivity, and achieving goals.

Living Successfully with ADHD

1

Create Structure

Use calendars, planners, and reminders to stay organized and on track with daily tasks.

2

Break Tasks Down

Divide large projects into smaller, manageable steps to avoid feeling overwhelmed.

3

Minimize Distractions

Create a quiet, organized workspace and use tools to block digital distractions.

4

Exercise Regularly

Physical activity helps improve focus, mood, and overall brain function.

5

Practice Self-Care

Prioritize sleep, nutrition, and stress management to support your mental health.

6

Build Support Networks

Connect with family, friends, support groups, and healthcare professionals.
